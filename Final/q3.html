For xt less than or equal to km/kp, there is only one steady state solution, which is at xStar = 0. The forward and reverse rates are zero in this case, and the state is stable because increasing xStar from zero results in a higher dephosphrylation rate than phoshorylation rate.

For xt > km/kp, there are two steady state values of xStar: xStar = 0, and xStar = xt -km/kp. The former is unstable while the latter is stable.

Ultrasenstivity refers to the large change in output in response to a small change in input. This occurs for the input values around xt = km/kp, where bifurcation occurs. Steady-state Xstar = 0 for all xt < km/kp, but a small increase in xt just below km/kp to xt > km/kp will create a nonzero output that increases linearly with xt.
